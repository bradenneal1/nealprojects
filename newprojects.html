Completed Projects

Reilly - Whitianga

Designed 2011 & built 2012, continuing into 2013. A Jazz Cafe downstairs with owners apartment upstairs. A large building of over 500m2 on a corner beachfront site in Whitianga. Concrete tilt slab construction was used for the main structure for several reasons. Quickness of construction, fire rating between the two tenancies,weather tightness & earthquake resistance. Unispan midfloor system was used in conjunction with steel beams. A conventional timber framed construction was used for the top storey. Asphalt tiles on plywood for the roof. Cedar weatherboards for the remainder of the cladding. The concrete tilt slabs have a plastered & painted surface, while the 3 full height panels are coloured aggregate finish.

Beer - Whitianga

Designed early 2009 and built that same year. The site of only 400m2 with maximum site coverage of only 140m2. However a practical home with all amenities was achieved. Open plan living with 1 bedroom & ensuite downstairs, along with a double garage & laundry. Upstairs has 2 bedrooms with a shared bathroom.. The stairs brings you to an open study & family area. All this in 200m2 + 10m2 deck. This in turn has access to the sun filled deck, as does 1 of the bedrooms. The roofing is asphalt tiles on plywood. The cladding is a combination of Linear weatherboards & plastered polystyrene. There is also some feature stonework of the front of the dwelling.

Bonner - Whitianga

Basement double garage, with games room, laundry & storage A concrete midfloor providing a large deck, finished in coloured concrete. Gayford - Tairua

Designed late 2004 & built over a number of years. A large home of over 300m2 plus 80m2 of decks. All concrete construction, with Firth Hotbloc for most walls & Unispan concrete floor system. A complicated house with at least 10 different floor levels throughout the three floors. Even the stairs from the basement to the top are insitu concrete. It also has a glass carrier lift. The kitchen benches are also made from coloured concrete. A blockwork swimming pool completes this home by the sea.

 

Henry - Hahei

Designed early 2006 & built 2006/2007. A huge house of over 500m2 plus 125m2 of decks. A steep site overlooking Hahei, with well established trees which had to be protected thru out the building process. With restrictive access to the site, a large retaining wall at the bottom was constructed first. Then the swimming pool, after which the house itself started! The basement consists of 2 bedrooms, bathroom , laundry, large family room with bar, cellar and a sauna. A concrete midfloor to the lounge dining kitchen & main bedroom with ensuite all opening onto the decks. Behind these rooms are a guest bedroom with bathroom facilities and an entertainment room. Upstairs is a office/study and the entrance which is accessed via a bridge from the driveway. A lift services all floors,but the curved staircase from the entry to the lounge area holds centre stage. High quality materials were used throughout this project.

Jackson - Whitianga

Designed late 2004/early 2005 Built 2005 by owner. 254m2. Steel frame to the lounge, dining kitchen area. Concrete block wall runs the full length of the house through the axis acting as a heatsink. This results in a very warm house. All the service rooms are behind this wall gaining heat. Exterior cladding consists of a mixture of linear weatherboards & titan board with negative detail. A very user friendly layout with the lounge/dining/kitchen area as the central hub. The master bedroom & ensuite to one side & the two other bedrooms & bathroom on the other.

 

Kite - Whitianga

Designed 2009 & built 2009/2010. A concrete tilt slab construction of approx 250m2. The ground floor has double garage with laundry/bathroom, kitchen, dining, lounge, 2 bedrooms with a shared bathroom. Upstairs has the main bedroom with ensuite. An office space is at the stair lobby. A feature is the concrete cantilevered stair treads. Also the long kitchen bench is also of coloured concrete. High sloping ceilings to the lounge, dining & kitchen give a spacious feel. The use of timber for the dining/kitchen ceilings add some warmth to the concrete house, while the same timber was used for the kitchen & bathroom fittings to give a connected look. This is a very warm, maintenance free home

Vickers - Whitianga

A large house deigned in 2007 and meticulously built by the owner over a number of years. It consists of 312m2 of house plus 44m2 of boat canopy. During construction a 25m2 music room upstairs was added in 2009. A double garage with a separate workshop, 3 bedrooms & study plus a large open kitchen/dining/lounge.
